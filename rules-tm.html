My Chore & Screen Time Rules

These rules are here to help things go smoothly every day — for everyone!

Why were these rules created?
These rules were made because some of your recent behavior and choices needed to be addressed. At the same time, the people who care about you also understand that gaming is important — sometimes you need extra time to finish projects, complete activities, or play with your friends online. These rules are meant to be fair. They give you a clear path to earn the screen time you want, and they help everyone know exactly what to expect each day.
🧹
Section 1 — Daily Chores
  • 1
    Every day, you have 19 chores to complete. These are easy chores — nothing too hard or too long.
  • 2
    To unlock screen time for the day, you must complete all 19 chores. That is your daily minimum.
  • 3
    Finishing all 19 chores comes with a special rewards! (See Section 2 and 6.)
⏱️
Section 2 — Daily Screen Time
  • 1
    Once you have finished all 19 chores and maintained good behaviors, you unlock 2 hours of screen time for that day.
  • 2
    If you have done fewer than 19 chores, there is no screen time for that day.
  • 3
    Screen time includes video games, TV, phone, and the Switch — all these screens count toward the 2-hour limit.
🌟
Section 3 — Weekend Bonus Screen Time
  • 1
    On the weekend, you have the chance to earn 2 extra hours of bonus screen time — on top of your regular 2 hours!
  • 2
    To earn this bonus, two things must both be true:
    ✅  Your chores were done consistently all week long.
    ✅  Your behavior was good throughout the week.
  • 3
    This bonus was added because we know that sometimes you need more time in games — like when you’re working on a big project or want to hang out with friends online.
🌅
Section 4 — Saturday Special Rules
  • 1
    Saturday is a special day with its own set of rules. You only have to complete 8 chores on Saturday — numbers 1, 2, 10, 11, 12, 15, 17, and 18. That’s it. No other chores are required that day. However, you must also maintain good behaviors A through E.
  • 2
    These 8 chores can be done before the sun sets, which means you can unlock your screen time earlier — no long wait!
  • 3
    Once your 8 Saturday chores are done, you unlock your 2 hours of screen time for the day, just like any other day — plus your 2 extra hours, if you earned them.
  • 4
    On Saturdays, your screens can stay on as late as 10:00 PM if you are quiet and respectful. This means if your screen time is still going at 9 PM, you do not have to stop. You can keep going until your time runs out or 10 PM arrives, whichever comes first.
  • 5
    Important: The 10 PM rule is about screens — not bedtime. If your screen time runs out before 10 PM and after 9 PM, that is when you go to bed. You do not get to stay up just because it is Saturday. After 9 PM, When your screens go off, you go to bed.
  • 6
    This is a firm rule — no ifs, ands, or buts. The 10 PM cutoff will not be extended further.
💰
Section 5 — Your Weekly Money Reward
  • 1
    If you complete all 24 quests every day for the full week, you will earn $4.00 at the end of that week. This money is yours to keep!
  • 2
    You can spend your money on things like LEGO sets, video games, digital currency (such as Robux or V-Bucks), or other toys — as long as they are approved by your mother first.
  • 3
    The best part? Every dollar you save makes your next purchase even better. Instead of spending right away, try saving up — because the things you really want cost more, and the feeling of buying them yourself is awesome.

Section 7 — Mom's Quiet Hours
  • 1
    Every single day — from 9:00 PM to 9:00 AM — Mom is not to be disturbed. This applies every night, no exceptions.
  • 2
    This is her personal, private time. She needs this rest in order to take care of herself — and to take care of you. It is not negotiable and it is not something she should have to ask for. It is hers.
  • 3
    During these hours, do not knock on her door, call for her, text her, or try to get her attention for any reason — unless it is a real emergency.
  • 4
    What counts as a real emergency? The examples below not the only emergencies that exist, and this list does not limit what counts as one. Use your best judgment. When in doubt, ask yourself: "Could someone get seriously hurt if I wait until morning?" If yes — that is likely an emergency.
    🚨 Examples of Real Emergencies
    🔥 There is a fire or smoke in the house
    🩸 Someone is badly hurt and/or bleeding
    🤢 You are seriously sick or in real pain
    🌊 There is a flood or water leak
    🚪 Someone strange is trying to get inside
    ⚡ A dangerous power or electrical problem
  • 5
    What is NOT an emergency — being bored, wanting a snack, having a question, not being able to sleep, wanting to show her something, or anything that can safely wait until 9 AM. If it can wait, it must wait.
  • 6
    Mom works hard every day to take care of you and your home. These hours are how she recharges. Respecting her quiet time is one of the most important things you can do for her. 💗
⚖️
Section 8 — The Council
  • 1
    These rules are firm. That means there are no compromises in the moment — no negotiating, no arguing, no trying to change the rules on the spot.
  • 2
    However, if you have a concern about a rule — like something feels unfair or doesn’t make sense — you have the right to bring it to The Council.
  • 3
    The Council is actually just Eon, lol. He can be reached by text message at (555) 555-5555. A concern may also be presented in person.
  • 4
    Before The Council will consider any issue, two people must both share their side of the story — you and your mother. Eon needs to hear both perspectives before he can make a fair decision.
  • 5
    Once both sides have been heard, The Council will decide whether a compromise should be considered. His decision is final.
  • 6
    The right way to share a concern is calmly and respectfully — not by arguing or getting upset. Bringing a concern to The Council is always okay. Ignoring the rules is not.

These rules were made because we care about you and want things to go well — for you and for everyone around you. ❤️

Do your chores, be kind, and enjoy your screen time. You’ve got this! ⭐
